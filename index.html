Coach Reference Guide BIP-informed training prescription framework
Core Principle

Match play is characterised by high-frequency, short-burst, fast-restart sequences. The BIP Profile Index (BPI) measures how closely a training drill replicates the structural profile of competitive NPL match play across three dimensions: sequence length distribution, restart pressure, and session density. A score of 100 = structurally identical to match.

BPI Score Zones

ScoreZoneInterpretationTypical Context
75–100Match-ApproximateStructurally close to match across all three dimensions11v11, BPO drill (fast restart), near-full sided MD-1/MD-2
50–74Match-AdjacentSimilar in some dimensions, deviating in othersATT v DEF (sustained BIP), 7v7 possession, structured SSG
30–49Tactical/StructuralSignificant deviation. Tactically valuable but physiologically dissimilarPossession circuits, shape work, SSGs with coaching interruption
0–29Intensity IsolatedHigh intensity per sequence, very different structural profileWave games, 1v1s, finishing, set piece

Drill Archetypes

TYPE AIntensity Overload
High output per sequence, long BOP. Trains neuromuscular quality, speed, individual actions. Does not train the repeated-effort-with-short-recovery pattern of match play.
Wave Games, 1v1s, finishing circuits, unopposed speed work
TYPE BMatch-Approximate
Both intensity and restart pressure near match demands. Most achievable in large-sided formats with deliberate ball management. Currently rare in training data.
BPO drill (fast restart), 11v11 full pitch, ATT v DEF at MD-1/MD-2
TYPE CVolume / Structure
Moderate output, long BOP, often with coaching interruptions. Tactically necessary. BOP target here prevents interruptions from undermining tactical learning.
Possession games, 4v4+4, shape/unit work, 7v6 tactical

Prescription Parameters

1 · Restart Mode (BOP Target)
PRESS — BOP ≤20s
Balls immediately returned. No coaching stoppages. Match or faster restart.
NORM — BOP 20–35s
Match-equivalent restart. Spare balls at perimeter. Brief verbal feedback only.
RECOVERY — BOP >35s
Intentional extended recovery. Coaching focus. Tactical learning priority.
2 · Sequence Profile (BIP Bin Focus)
SHORT-BURST — ≥60% in 0–30s
Transition, press, counter-press, quick restart scenarios.
MATCH-PROFILE — ≥50% in 15–60s
Mirrors NPL match distribution. Primary target for MD-1/MD-2 drills.
SUSTAINED — ≥40% in 60–120s
Longer possession, conditioning overload, structural work.
